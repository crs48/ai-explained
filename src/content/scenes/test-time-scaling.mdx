The breakthrough behind reasoning models is **test-time compute scaling**: you can
make a model better not only by training it more, but by letting it **think for
longer when you ask it a question**.

OpenAI's o1 showed this follows a clean **log-linear** curve — accuracy rises
roughly *linearly* as you spend *exponentially* more thinking compute. Drag the
budget slider in the panel to move up the curve.

The most striking demonstration is open: **DeepSeek-R1-Zero**, trained with pure
reinforcement learning, climbed from **15.6% to 71.0%** on the AIME 2024 math
competition — and it did so by *learning on its own to write longer and longer
reasoning*.

> One honest caveat, worth toggling: this clean scaling depends on having a
> **verifier** — some way to check whether an answer is actually right (a math
> checker, a code test). Turn the verifier off and the gains get murky. That's
> why math and code drove the first reasoning models.

So the model needs to generate a long chain of thought — and *keep reading its own
thoughts as it goes*. Let's look at that loop directly.

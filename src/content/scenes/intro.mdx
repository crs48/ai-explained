Everything a large language model does comes down to answering **one question**,
over and over: *given all the text so far, what token comes next?*

That's it. ChatGPT, Claude, DeepSeek, o3 — under the hood they are all machines
that read a sequence and output a probability distribution over the next little
chunk of text. Then they append one and do it again.

A **reasoning model** (like OpenAI's o-series or DeepSeek-R1) is the *same
machine*. It isn't a new kind of neural network. The difference is that it was
trained to **think out loud to itself for a long time before answering** — and
that turns out to be startlingly powerful.

To see why, we'll walk the pipeline every LLM shares — **tokenize → embed →
attention → sample** — with things you can poke at each step. Then we'll add the
one loop that makes a reasoning model reason.

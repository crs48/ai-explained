Remember those conditioning vectors from the text encoder? Here's where they do
their job. At every denoising step, the image talks to your prompt through
**cross-attention**.

In the LLM track, self-attention had tokens attending to *other tokens* — Queries,
Keys, and Values all came from one sequence. **Cross-attention is the same math**
(Q·Kᵀ → softmax → weighted V) with one twist:

- the **image latent** provides the **Queries** ("what should this region look
  like?"), and
- the **text embeddings** provide the **Keys and Values** ("here's what the words
  mean").

So each patch of the image "looks up" which words are relevant and pulls in their
meaning. Hover the prompt words in the panel: **"balloon"** lights up the balloon,
**"hills"** the hills. (The regions here are illustrative, but this is genuinely
how the prompt controls *where* things go.)

One knob remains: how *hard* the model should listen to your prompt.
